# news

**2021.06.20** - At 6pm Eastern, music from *okay ohio* and *ednes* will be featured on a new podcast on [WFIU](https://indianapublicmedia.org/radio/). *How to Survive the Future* is the brain child of IU professor [Alex Chambers](https://alexchambers.net/) for an Indiana Humanities initiative called [*Unearthed*](https://indianahumanities.org/programs/unearthed/). According to Chambers, it can be thought of as "oral histories from the future. We ask a range of people around Indiana to talk about today from the perspective of tomorrow, in particular about social challenges we're facing today. But it's not about 'solving problems' per se. It's more about exploring ideas and feelings about how the current moment feels, with the challenges we face, and how things might look differently down the road." It's a beautiful idea, and I'm ecstatic to play a part in its conception.

**2021.06.03** - At 7pm Eastern, Grant McClintock from the Musical Family Tree radio show on [WQRT](https://www.wqrt.org/) aired an Indiana Composers special, featuring interviews with myself and two incredibly talented local composers, [Rob Funkhouser](https://www.robfunkhouser.com/) and [Mina Keohane](https://minakeohane.com/). You can find an archived recording of the show on their [mixcloud page](https://www.mixcloud.com/WQRT_Indianapolis/). I was absolutely humbled to be involved in this.
